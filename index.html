OT Cybersecurity Software: An Independent Guide

The OT security market has matured fast and fragmented badly. This site maps the vendor landscape, separates platform plays from specialist tools, and gives practitioners the analysis they need to build a defensible shortlist — without reading marketing copy.

What this site covers

OT cybersecurity software spans a wide range of functions: asset visibility, threat detection, network segmentation, vulnerability management, and secure remote access. The vendor landscape cuts across pure-play OT specialists, IT security giants extending into industrial environments, and a growing tier of challengers solving specific problems better than any platform vendor bothers to.

Every section of this site is organized around a practitioner decision, not a product category. The goal is to help you figure out what kind of tool you need before you start making calls.

Why independent

Most OT security content is produced by vendors, funded by vendors, or written by analysts whose methodology is opaque. This site has no vendor relationships, no sponsored content, and no affiliate arrangements with the platforms it covers. Analysis is based on publicly available information, practitioner input, and direct product evaluation where possible.

When this site has an opinion, it says so. When the evidence is thin, it says that too.
